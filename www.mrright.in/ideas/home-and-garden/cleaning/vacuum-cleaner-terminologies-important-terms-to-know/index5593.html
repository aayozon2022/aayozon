Vacuum Cleaner Terminologies: Important Terms to Know

Rohan Tyagi
9 months ago

It can be really hard to understand certain words that you are not familiar with in the Vacuum cleaning world. Hence, we have made this glossary with the most common vacuum cleaner terminologies. These terms will help you to understand if you want to buy a vacuum cleaner or want to get it repaired.

1. Air Flow

The motion of air starts with one area then onto the next, generally estimated in CFM (cubic feet each moment). It is the power applied by the moving air which really gets the dust and moves it into the pack or dust holder. This is one of the main parts of deciding the genuine proportion of vacuum cleaner efficiency.

2. Allergen

A substance that can cause an unfavorably susceptible allergy to the body. Normal allergens incorporate dust, creature dander, dust vermin, and fungi.

3. Amperage (amps)

This is the most misjudged and least solid strategy for deciding the efficiency of a vacuum cleaner. The allowed amperage permitted by UL is 12 amps. We have vacuums that shift from 4.2 amps to 12 amps.
However, it is just a limit to which the cleaner is confined. This is the MAXIMUM rating. Amperage DOES NOT rise to execution.
Also, don’t get confused between Amperage and ampere as both terms are different.

4. Ampere

An ampere is the SI unit of electric current. The flow of current in any electrical appliance or system is measured in amperes.
When 1 coulomb of charge is passed through 1 second of time, it is considered as 1 ampere.

5. Air Watts

An air watt is a widely accepted unit of power to calculate the output strength of a vacuum cleaner. Do not take it as regular watts as watt is the input power of the vacuum cleaner.

6. Bagless Vacuum Cleaner

All cordless vacuum cleaners are bagless, with the goal that dust is caught inside a canister and discharged into a receptacle. Therefore, It saves the customer from exchanging the packs again and again.

7. Brushless DC Motor

A brushless engine is a DC electric engine with a durable magnet rotor and a rotating magnetic stator. In contrast to a brushed engine, it doesn’t have to have sliding contacts, that is, brushes, on the engine shaft to work. A brushless engine applies a lower mechanical opposition, wiping out the chance of sparkles shaping as the pivot speed increments. Hence, decreases extensively the requirement for intermittent support.

8. Cordless Vacuum Cleaner

A lightweight and battery-operated vacuum cleaner. A cordless vacuum cleaner is a compact version of conventional cleaners. However, It works efficiently as effectively as any other vacuum cleaner. Hence, a cordless vacuum cleaner becomes an ideal choice for cars and other hidden places.

9. Crevice Tool

It’s the tiniest accessory of a vacuum cleaner. It has a lean shape and a bent tip. Therefore, making it helpful to get into corners, baseboards, around radiators or vents, between couches, and so on.

10. Decibel (dB)

It is the unit used to estimate the power of a sound. On its scale, the lowest hearable sound – close to an all-out quietness – is 0dB. However, A sound multiple times all the more remarkable is 10dB.
For instance, all-out quiet is around 0dB. A murmur is 15dB. An ordinary discussion is around 60dB. A lawnmower is 90dB. A vehicle horn is 110dB. A stage performance is 120dB. A shot is 140dB.
Hence, it is an important factor to look for while buying a vacuum cleaner to know its maximum producing sound.

11. Filter

A paper, material, or delicate plastic surface with bunches of small openings that permit the air to be pushed through preventing the dust from passing. It is required so the residue and particles that the vacuum sucks along with air don’t get removed back out of the vacuum.
Therefore, it is necessary to keep replacing the filters in your vacuum cleaner with time.

12. HEPA

HEPA stands for High-Efficiency Particulate Air and it is one of the most searched vacuum cleaner terminologies on the internet. It is a grade of filter that is a universally accepted standard. Hence, it becomes necessary for a decent vacuum cleaner to be enabled with a HEPA filter.
According to the rule, a HEPA filter is capable of stopping 99.97% of the dust particles equal to or bigger than 0.3 microns. Hence, making it the most efficient filter to date.

13. Microns

The estimation that is utilized in vacuum cleaners. The millionth piece of a meter.
To place this into viewpoint, the width of a human hair is regularly 80 to 100 microns.
The term is regularly utilized in depicting the spans of particles held via air and water filters and for determining molecule sizes for the HEPA filtration particulars.

14. Revolutions per minute (rpm)

You might be familiar with the term if you have a vehicle at your home. However, it surprisingly has the same meaning everywhere.
It is to determine the number of cycles a machine/engine in one minute. Hence, the higher the rpm, the higher will be the power of the machine.

15. S-Class

One more name for a HEPA filter. It is what could be compared to the HEPA particular and demonstrates that a channel fulfills tough guidelines like the British Standard 5415 and German standard: DIN 2418B Ti-E EU 13 for filtration efficiency.

16. Suction

The capacity of a vacuum cleaner to effectively pull the dust in. It alludes to the real draw or tension contrast made by the turning fans in the suction engine. The suction of a vacuum cleaner is measured in ‘inches of water lift’.

17. Upholstery Tool

An accessory of the vacuum cleaner, it is ideal for pulling out dust from couches, seats, beddings, and pads.

18. Upright Vacuum Cleaner

one of the most widely searched vacuum cleaner terminologies. A sort of vacuum cleaner that has the engine and the pulling head in a single unit. You need to push the vacuum before you and don’t need to lean towards the floor or the surface to clean.

19. Volume

How much dust the vacuum cleaner can hold at a given instance. The volume is usually estimated in liters.

20. Watts

Wattage is defined as the power that is driven into the vacuum engine. The higher the wattage is, the more power and result from the vacuum cleaner.
