How to take care of a dengue patient- As advised by Doctors

Shipra Mangla
5 years ago

Dengue is a disease which is caused when an infected Aedes mosquito bites humans. It is a fact that there is no cure or vaccine for treating dengue. The treatment usually focuses on relieving the symptoms and effects. If someone in your family has contracted dengue then it is very important that you take care of him properly. Rest, good diet and proper medication hold the key to recover from the condition. This article focuses on how to take care of a dengue patient as advised by doctors. Here goes!

 

1. Maintain a clean and hygienic environment at home

The first thing that you must do to take care of a dengue patient as advised by doctors is to maintain a clean and hygienic environment at home. Also, make sure the environment is mosquito free since the infection can spread from person to person through mosquitoes.  For this you can do the following:

  • Use screens on doors and windows
  • Use mosquito nets
  • Make use of mosquito repellents in and around the house.
  • Avoid water collection in any area in and around the house as mosquitoes breed in stagnant water.

Image Credits: housebeautiful.com

2. Make sure patient gets enough rest and proper medical consultation

When the symptoms are noticed at first, make sure you get a proper test done to diagnose the problem. Once detected, consult a good doctor and get a proper medication chart and diet plan from the doctor. It is important to take the dengue patient to the doctor everyday so as to monitor the fever and other symptoms. Moreover, the patient must get enough rest and sleep since dengue can cause a lot of fatigue and tiredness. The patient must also be given all the medicines suggested by the doctor in a timely manner.

Image Credits: wisegeek.com

3. Plenty of fluids should be given

Another way to take care of a dengue patient as advised by doctors is giving them a lot of fluids from time to time. Water, fruit juices, soups and other fluids prove good as they prevent dehydration and also flush out the toxins from the body.  You can also prepare a juice by making use of papaya leaves and give this to dengue patients once or twice a day.  Papaya leaves help to increase the platelet count which thus helps in recovering from the disease.

Image Credits: Wikipedia

 

4. Give proper diet

 

Dengue patients must be given a good diet in order to recover fast. The diet must include foods and vegetables rich in Vitamin C, coconut water, herbal teas, vegetables juices, porridge etc.  Neem leaves, oranges and soups too are considered good for dengue patients. Remember not to give them any oily or spicy foods as these can be bad for the stomach which is a problem area among dengue patients.

Image Credits: mmm.sekidot.com.info

 

5. Monitor the symptoms daily

It is important to monitor the condition of the patient as well as all the symptoms on a daily basis. Make a chart of the fluid intake, progress made by the patient, number of times urine passed, fever and other such things on a daily basis as these can prove useful for the doctor to give a more appropriate treatment to the patient.

Image Credits: Flickr

6. Take the patient to the hospital if symptoms worsen

If the symptoms worsen, then it is important for you to take the patient to the hospital immediately. Cases in which this must be done include :

  • persistent vomiting
  • severe abdominal pain
  • inability to pass urine regularly
  • bleeding from any part of the body
  • difficulty in breathing etc.

Now that you have read how to take care of a dengue patient as advised by doctors, you are more equipped to help a family member or friend suffering from it. Prevention is better than cure and this is why you must avoid all possibilities of suffering from this condition.
