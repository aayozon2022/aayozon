Top Tips For Beginner Woodworkers

Abeniel Huten
4 years ago

Are you new to woodworking? If so, you may have a steep learning curve ahead of you! If you’re a beginner woodworker it can be an exciting yet slightly worrying time. Woodwork is a wonderfully rewarding hobby or career, however, it can be a dangerous one if you aren’t careful. It can also be confusing to learn a new skill, especially one as complicated as working with wood, so here are some top tips to help you to get started and to get the most out of your new pastime.

Don’t Make It Too Difficult At First

Always start out with a simple project that you can easily achieve success. If you begin with a very complex task, you’ll soon become frustrated because you don’t yet have the skill to get the job done. Something modest and simple will be more likely to succeed without disappointment or frustration and you’ll get the self-confidence boost of knowing you’ve done the job well. There are lots of stages in any successful woodworking project from designing and researching to choosing the right wood and tools, and actually working on the piece. You need to learn how to progress through each stage and know how they relate to each other before being able to start work on something more difficult.

Choose The Right Wood

No woodwork project will be its best if you use the wrong wood. Successful woodworkers choose the right wood for each project and prepare it properly. Unlike clay, glass or metal, wood is a biological property and it is therefore varied. Every tree, board, and species is completely different and those differences really matter. You need to take the time to learn about different types of wood and which are best for each type of project. You should try to work with as many different types as you can so you can gain as broad an experience as possible.

Choose The Right Tools

Woodworkers need a lot of tools. It’s important to choose good quality ones. While buying a large set of new tools can be costly and intimidating, especially for a newcomer to the hobby, it’s important to remember that they need to be functional and practical. You don’t need to buy everything all at once. Instead, choose a project, work out which tools you need to get the job done and then buy only those tools at first. Don’t fall into the trap of buying poor quality tools. They may be cheap but they won’t last and they won’t achieve good results. It’s also more important to choose versatile tools rather than specialized gadgets. Avoid those which advertise themselves as requiring little skill to use. Learn instead how to properly use a standard good quality tool so that you become confident at using it. That’s the only way to develop true woodworking skills.

Use A Range Of Resources

There are so many different resources out there for learning about woodwork. You can use books, attend classes or even use the internet. Always choose a reliable source so you can be sure to learn the best possible way of carrying out every job. However, remember that sometimes you’ll need to adapt methods a little to suit your own skills or a particular way of working. As long as the end result is good, that doesn’t matter.

Be Proud Of Your Craft

When you’ve made something with your new woodworking skills, take the time to appreciate it. Put it in your home and admire it regularly. Don’t be afraid to invest money in your new hobby if it’s important to you. After all, it will bring a lot of pleasure into your life and allow you to learn valuable and practical skills which will stand you in good stead for achieving all kinds of goals.
